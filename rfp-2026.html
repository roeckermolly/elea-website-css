National Request for Proposals 2026

Elea Institute invites qualified organizations to apply for funding through our 2026 National grantmaking cycle. We are committed to partnering with organizations that expand access to hospice and palliative care, advance research and education, and address unmet needs in serious illness care across the United States.

Up to $3M
Total awards distributed to between five and ten organizations during the 2026 grantmaking cycle.

Focus Areas

Elea Institute's 2026 grantmaking is organized around four aspirational pillars. Organizations whose work aligns with one or more of these areas are encouraged to apply.

Pillar 1

Improving Access to Quality Care

Enhancing access to serious illness care for underserved populations, including expanding geographic reach, reducing disparities, and removing systemic barriers.

Pillar 2

Advancing Research, Education, and Training

Building professional capabilities among caregivers, clinicians, and healthcare teams through evidence-based research, clinical training, and educational resources.

Pillar 3

Increasing End-of-Life Care Awareness

Advancing public understanding of hospice and palliative care options through national research, awareness campaigns, and advocacy initiatives.

Pillar 4

Unmet Needs & Emerging Solutions

Supporting innovative and transformative ideas that address unmet needs in serious illness care, including new models of care delivery and technology-enabled solutions.

Selection Criteria

Elea Institute evaluates all proposals against the following criteria:

  • Demonstrated financial stability and responsible stewardship of grant funds
  • Direct, measurable impact on patients, caregivers, or the quality of care provided
  • Preference for new initiatives or meaningful expansions of existing programs
  • A clear financial sustainability plan that demonstrates long-term viability beyond the grant period

Eligibility Requirements

To be considered for funding, organizations must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code
  • Directly engaged in palliative care, hospice care, or related serious illness fields
  • Operating programs within the United States
  • Committed to increasing the number of patients served and improving the quality of care delivered

Exclusions

The following organizations or uses of funds are not eligible for consideration:

  • For-profit organizations (except those with a clearly defined charitable purpose and appropriate board oversight)
  • Organizations with operating budgets where more than 25% of costs are unrelated to the proposed program or core mission
  • Grant funds may not be used to support direct lobbying activities
