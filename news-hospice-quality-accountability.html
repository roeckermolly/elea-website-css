Hospice at Its Best: Quality, Access, and Accountability

Hospice and palliative services play an important role in supporting patients and families facing terminal conditions and serious illness. As reports highlighting fraud, waste, and abuse have received increased attention, it is important to remember high-quality hospice providers deliver meaningful support that improves patient experience and aligns treatment with individual goals.

Elea Institute supports strong oversight that protects patients and ensures our limited government program resources are directed to those who qualify for hospice services. Accountability is central to our mission and essential to maintaining public trust and long-term stability.

At the same time, the broader picture must remain in focus. Many eligible patients are still referred too late to meaningfully benefit from hospice services, and disparities in utilization persist across communities. Efforts to strengthen quality and compliance should move forward alongside strategies that promote earlier referrals, improve education, and reduce barriers to hospice care access.

Elea Institute is focused on advancing practical, evidence-based solutions that strengthen hospice delivery and expand appropriate access. Through cross-sector partnerships, Elea Institute works to identify gaps in utilization and support approaches that help patients receive appropriate and beneficial palliative and hospice care services at the right time.

Reinforcing accountability, strengthening quality, and broadening access are interconnected priorities. Advancing these goals together ensures hospice services remain responsive, equitable, and positioned to meet the needs of a growing population.
